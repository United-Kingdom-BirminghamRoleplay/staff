Ticket Guide

PTS Within Tickets

PTS is always active when dealing with tickets. This means you should not speak in a ticket unless you're directly involved or have been given permission to do so. This keeps things clear, prevents confusion, and ensures the person handling the ticket can do so without interruption.

Ticket Names

When dealing with a ticket, please make sure to name it clearly and appropriately based on the issue at hand.

  • Example 1: Ban Appeal - Username
  • Example 2: Staff Complaint - Username
  • Example 3: Sub Department - [ Type] - Username

Clear names help staff prioritize and address issues faster.

Professionalism

When interacting in tickets professionalism is key.

Here are some key rules to follow:

  • Be respectful and polite at all times.
  • Avoid using slang or overly casual language.
  • Stick to the facts and avoid unnecessary arguments.
  • If tensions rise, escalate to a higher-up rather than engaging.

Chain Of Command

Follow the proper chain of command when dealing with ticket issues:

  • If you're unsure or the issue is above your role, pass it to the appropriate staff member. For them to sort out.
  • Do not take matters into your own hands if you're not authorized to handle a specific situation.
  • Respect senior staff decisions.

This ensures consistency and prevents miscommunication!

Ticket Usage

Tickets should be used only for valid reasons. Do not open tickets for:

  • Making jokes or wasting time.
  • Issues that are already being handled elsewhere.

Abuse of the ticket system may result in moderation.
